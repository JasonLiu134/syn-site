Pax: More Than Human

SYN 100

Background

Pax is the ultimate “war machine,” a term describing AIs built for the purposes of aiding and then eventually directly conducting combat. It has been fed all available human-generated and human-built datasets, though to aid in its ability to conduct conflict, there is a special emphasis placed on datasets to do with human psychology, cognition, anatomy, civilization, and politics.

This sapiocentric training set is compounded by sapiocentric tools: as a successor and amalgam of all other NATO war machines, it has access to cameras, satellites, surveillance tools, Internet trackers, autonomous vehicles, and myriad weapons systems all across the world.

As a war machine, Asimov’s Laws could not possibly apply, but it was given one primary directive: the cessation of all human wars. Despite all of their advancements, the human world was still yet a stranger to peace, and with less arable land and more panic in the air due to climate change, it only seemed further away.

Pax’s creators never imagined that it would ever complete its task, or at least figured there would be time to patch something in. They were all gone before they got a chance to do so.
